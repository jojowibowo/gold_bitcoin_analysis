6-Step Data Integrity Audit

To ensure "Operational Readiness," we executed a rigorous audit. This process transformed raw, noisy logs into high-fidelity dimensions for C-suite decision-making and legal auditing. Click on any step below to reveal the specific technical actions taken.

🗄️

1. Schema Unification

Addressed the "Data Silo Problem" using BigQuery UNION ALL logic.

Disparate naming conventions (e.g., last_status vs. message_status) were semantically mapped into a single status column, enabling cross-channel ROI comparisons.
⚠️
CRITICAL FIX

2. ID Truncation Fix

Remediated the Scientific Notation Bug in the ETL pipeline.

24-character hex Email IDs were incorrectly ingested as float64, truncating into scientific notation (e.g., 1.23E+23) and losing uniqueness. Scripts refactored to STRING, restoring 100% reconciliation accuracy.
✅

3. Null-Value Validation

Enforced zero-tolerance for NULLs in Critical Path columns.

Resolved "0.0" irregular values in revenue fields where is_charge was TRUE, correcting significant under-reporting biases in financial forecasts.
🧹

4. Duplicate Scrubbing

Executed rigorous full-row uniqueness validation for billing integrity.

Identified and removed redundant logs caused by retry-loop glitches in bulk SMS blasts, protecting client marketing budgets and averting contract terminations.
📈

5. Outlier Management

Distinguished "Power Users" from "System Noise" using IQR.

Separated legitimate high-frequency OTP traffic from API loop errors, ensuring dashboard visualizes "Clean Growth" rather than artificial traffic surges.
🏷️

6. Metadata Integration

Enriched transaction data via complex LEFT JOIN operations.

Connected cryptic transaction IDs to user_name and field_of_business. Moved analysis from "What happened" to "Who it happened to" for segmented health checks.

Root Cause Analysis (RCA)

Applying the Pattern → Anomaly → Impact → Hypothesis diagnostic framework uncovered hidden operational bottlenecks previously masked by siloed reporting. We identified critical issues in Voice Call routing and the severe financial impact of message latency.

The "Voice Call" Bottleneck

Anomaly: Success rates drop by 35% during Wed 18:00 WIB peaks among Finance clients due to carrier congestion/SIP-trunking limits.

Impact: Wasted infrastructure budget and degraded UX. Bank customers are left unable to authorize time-sensitive transactions.

Latency Sensitivity in Finance

Observation: A mere 5% dip in WA Read Rates correlates directly with a 12% slowdown in subsequent transaction volume.

Implication: Delivery speed is a direct driver of billable volume. Delayed OTPs cause high abandonment rates.

Strategic Recommendations & Roadmap

To capitalize on the analytical findings and drive long-term operational excellence, we propose a three-pronged strategy for the next fiscal quarter. This transforms our approach from reactive troubleshooting to proactive platform management.

💰

1. Predictive Credit & Burn-Rate Monitoring

Implement an automated alert system for Top 10 Enterprise users based on real-time hourly volume analysis.

  • Projects when prepaid balances will hit zero.
  • Triggers "Top-Up" notification 4 hours before service interruption.
  • Risk Mitigation: Prevents 4-7% loss in monthly billable volume from "Midnight Outages."
⚖️

2. Temporal Load Balancing (Traffic Tiering)

Deploy a dynamic queuing logic engine to mitigate the Wednesday 18:00 carrier congestion bottleneck.

  • Prioritize: "Transactional" traffic (Bank OTPs/2FA) through premium, low-latency gateways.
  • Queue: "Promotional" traffic automatically offset by 15-30 minutes.
  • Ensures high-stakes financial transactions are never delayed by marketing spam.
🔄

3. Channel Optimization & Migration

Incentivize Finance clients to migrate non-critical alerts from expensive, failing Voice trunks to WhatsApp.

  • WhatsApp offers higher success margins and verifiable "Read Rate" tracking.
  • Launch a "WhatsApp First" pilot program offering a 5% discount for 50% Voice volume transition.
  • Reduces carrier-level friction and lowers OCA's operational overhead.
